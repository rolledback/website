About Me


My name is Matthew Rayermann and I am senior at the University of Texas at Austin. I am currently in the pursuit of a B.S. in Computer Science and will be graduating in May 2016.

My exposure to programming began in middle school where I learned how to program TI calculators while not paying attention in math class. I later took formal CS classes while in high school and competed in various programming competitions (UIL and BPA) on the state and national level.

Here at UT Austin, I have taken a variety of CS courses and been active in a number of activities and student organizations including Texas ACM and the Longhorn Pep Band. I have also worked as an intern at Raytheon, Rackspace, and most recently Microsoft. Following graduation, I plan on travelling over the summer and then beginning my fulltime career as a software developer at Microsoft.

Some of my personal passions include travel, music (both listening and playing), cooking, and hiking.
